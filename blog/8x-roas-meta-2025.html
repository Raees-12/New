Why Chasing Low CPMs Is a Trap

The most common briefing we hear from brands switching agencies: "Our previous agency was getting us $4 CPMs." That sounds great — until you realise their conversion rate was 0.6% and their average order value was $40. The math doesn't work, regardless of how cheap the traffic is.

CPM (cost per thousand impressions) tells you how much you paid to show your ad. It tells you nothing about whether those people were ever going to buy. We optimise for purchase intent signals — audiences with demonstrated buying behaviour, high engagement rates with similar products, and strong match with your customer avatar.

The shift: Stop asking "how can I pay less per impression?" Start asking "how can I reach fewer, more qualified people who are actually going to buy?"

The Audience Architecture

Every Meta campaign we run uses a three-tier audience architecture. Each tier has a different job, a different creative strategy, and different success metrics.

01
Cold Audiences — Interest + Lookalike
We build cold audiences from three sources: interest stacks layered with behavioural signals, 1% lookalikes from your top 20% customers by LTV (not just purchasers), and broad audiences with a strong creative hook. Cold creative is educational, aspirational, or story-driven. We never lead with price or urgency at this stage.
02
Warm Audiences — Engaged Traffic
Website visitors (segmented by page depth), video viewers (25%+, 50%+, 75%+), Instagram engagers, and email list custom audiences. Warm creative uses social proof heavily — reviews, UGC, before/after. These people know you exist. The job is to convert consideration into intent.
03
Hot Audiences — High Intent Retargeting
Add-to-cart abandoners, checkout abandoners, product page visitors 3x+ in 7 days, and past purchasers for cross-sell. Hot creative is direct and specific — it references the exact product they viewed, uses urgency where authentic, and removes every possible friction from the conversion path.

The Creative Strategy That Actually Converts

Creative is the most important variable in a Meta campaign. Audience targeting matters. Campaign structure matters. But creative accounts for roughly 70% of performance variance in our experience.

We produce a minimum of 12 creatives per client per month, structured into three creative types:

  • Direct Response (40% of budget): Benefit-led, clear CTA, optimised for immediate purchase. Usually static image or 15-second video.
  • Social Proof (35% of budget): UGC, customer reviews, before/after, transformation stories. The highest-converting creative format for warm and hot audiences.
  • Brand Story (25% of budget): Longer-form content (30–60 seconds) that builds brand equity and feeds the top of funnel. These rarely convert immediately but they make every other ad perform better.

Campaign Structure: Simplified, Not Simplified

Meta's algorithm has become extremely powerful at finding buyers — if you give it the right objective and enough data. We've moved away from hyper-segmented campaign structures (one adset per audience, tightly controlled budgets) toward a CBO (Campaign Budget Optimisation) structure that lets Meta allocate spend dynamically across audiences.

Our typical campaign structure for a $20k+/month account: 1 Prospecting CBO campaign (3–4 adsets, 6–8 creatives), 1 Retargeting CBO campaign (2 adsets — warm and hot — 4–6 creatives), 1 Retention campaign targeting past purchasers. That's it. Simple is powerful when you have the right creative and the right offer.

The 5 Signals We Look for Before Scaling Spend

Scaling too early is one of the most common mistakes in paid advertising. When you increase budget before a campaign is stable, you force Meta's algorithm to re-enter the learning phase, CPMs spike, and performance tanks. We only scale when we see all five of these signals:

  1. 50+ purchase events in the last 7 days — the minimum for algorithmic stability
  2. ROAS above target for 7 consecutive days — not just one good day
  3. Frequency below 2.5 on winning adsets — audience not yet saturated
  4. Consistent creative performance — the same creative winning across multiple days
  5. Landing page conversion rate stable — if CVR drops, scaling spend just accelerates the problem

What We Track Every Week

Weekly reporting for every client covers: ROAS by campaign, creative performance ranked by purchase rate, audience frequency (saturation warning), cost per unique reach, and the metric most agencies ignore — new-to-brand customer acquisition rate. This last metric tells you whether your paid acquisition is building a real customer base or just re-selling to the same people.
