Why Email Still Wins in 2025

Every year someone declares email marketing dead. Every year the data proves them wrong. Email remains the highest-ROI marketing channel for e-commerce — not because it's glamorous, but because it reaches people who have already raised their hand and said they're interested in what you sell. No algorithm, no CPM, no paid distribution required.

The brands we work with who have strong email programmes consistently generate 30–40% of their total revenue from email alone — at near-zero incremental cost. Compared to Meta ads (where you pay for every conversion) and organic social (where reach is algorithm-dependent), email is the most reliable revenue engine in e-commerce.

The fundamental insight: Your email list is an asset you own. Your social following and your ad audiences are assets you rent. Every dollar you invest in growing and monetising your list compounds over time in a way that paid acquisition never will.

The 5 Core Flows Every E-Commerce Brand Needs

We build email programmes in two layers: core flows (automated sequences that run permanently) and campaigns (one-time sends to segments). For most brands, the flows alone — once built and optimised — will drive 25–35% of email revenue with zero ongoing effort.

01
Welcome Series (5 emails over 10 days)
The welcome series is the most important flow in your stack. These subscribers are at peak interest — they just opted in. Email 1: immediate delivery of any opt-in offer + brand story. Email 2 (day 2): social proof — your best reviews, press mentions, community. Email 3 (day 4): your best-selling product, no discount. Email 4 (day 7): an educational piece relevant to your product category. Email 5 (day 10): your first discount offer for anyone who hasn't purchased yet.
02
Abandoned Cart (3 emails over 24 hours)
Email 1 (1 hour after abandon): simple reminder, no discount. Just bring them back. Email 2 (12 hours): add social proof — reviews of the specific product they abandoned. Email 3 (24 hours): urgency + small incentive (5–10% off or free shipping). This sequence typically recovers 15–25% of abandoned carts.
03
Post-Purchase Flow (5 emails over 30 days)
Email 1: order confirmation (transactional, not promotional). Email 2 (day 3): onboarding/usage tips for the product. Email 3 (day 7): request for review. Email 4 (day 14): cross-sell to complementary products. Email 5 (day 30): repeat purchase prompt for consumables, or upsell for higher-tier products.
04
Browse Abandonment (2 emails over 48 hours)
Triggered when a subscriber views a product page but doesn't add to cart. Email 1 (4 hours): "Still thinking about [product]?" — low pressure. Email 2 (48 hours): social proof for that specific product category. Lower intent than cart abandon, but still converts at 3–5% and costs almost nothing to set up.
05
Win-Back Flow (3 emails over 60 days)
For subscribers who haven't opened an email in 90+ days. Email 1: curiosity subject line, no content preview. Email 2 (30 days): your best content or offer. Email 3 (60 days): "We're removing you from our list" — this creates urgency and typically has the highest open rate of the entire flow. Anyone who doesn't re-engage gets suppressed, keeping your list clean and deliverability high.

Segmentation: The Key to Scaling Email Revenue

Once your flows are built, segmentation is what takes email from good to exceptional. The segments we build for every client: by purchase history (1x, 2x, 3x+ buyers), by category interest (derived from browse and purchase data), by engagement level (active vs inactive), and by acquisition source (different welcome series for paid vs organic subscribers).

The Metrics That Actually Matter

Open rate is a vanity metric (especially since Apple Mail Privacy Protection). The metrics we track: click-to-open rate (CTOR), revenue per recipient, flow conversion rate, and list health (deliverability score and engagement rate). A list of 5,000 highly engaged subscribers will generate more revenue than a list of 50,000 mostly inactive ones.
