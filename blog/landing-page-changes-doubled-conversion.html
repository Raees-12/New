12 Landing Page Changes That Doubled Our Client's Conversion Rate in 30 Days

You can have the best ads in the world, but if your landing page converts at 1%, you're throwing money away. Here's exactly what we changed — and what moved the needle most.

Priya Sharma
Head of Strategy

The Audit Process

Before we change anything on a landing page, we understand what's happening. We install heatmapping (Hotjar), run session recordings for 48 hours, and analyse the Google Analytics funnel to identify exactly where people are dropping off. For this client, 68% of visitors were leaving within 8 seconds of landing. That's an above-the-fold problem, not a checkout problem.

Rule: Never guess at CRO. The data will always show you where the problem is. Assumptions cost money.

Above the Fold: The First 8 Seconds

The above-fold section of a landing page does one job: give the visitor a clear reason to stay. We made four changes here:

01
Replaced brand photography with product-in-use imagery
The hero image was a beautiful, abstract lifestyle photo with no product visible. We replaced it with a clear product shot showing the item in use by a person who looks like the target customer. Click-through to product pages increased 34%.
02
Added a benefit headline above the product name
The original headline was the product name. We added a one-line benefit statement above it: "The only [product] that [specific benefit]." Immediately communicates value before the customer has to do any reading.
03
Moved the star rating and review count above the fold
This was below the fold in the original design. Moving 4.8 stars and "2,341 reviews" to immediately below the headline increased add-to-cart rate by 18%.
04
Reduced page load time from 4.2s to 1.8s
Compressed images, deferred non-critical scripts, and removed three unnecessary third-party plugins. Every 100ms improvement in load time correlates with roughly 1% improvement in conversion rate on mobile.

Social Proof: Making It Impossible to Doubt

The client had 2,300 reviews averaging 4.8 stars — an incredible asset they were barely using. We made four changes to surface this proof throughout the page:

  • Added a review carousel immediately below the hero (not buried at the bottom)
  • Selected 3 reviews specifically addressing the most common purchase objections
  • Added a "Verified Purchase" badge to each review
  • Added a "As seen in" press bar featuring any media mentions

Removing Checkout Friction

The checkout process had seven form fields. We reduced it to four (name, email, address, payment). We also added three trust signals at checkout: SSL badge, money-back guarantee icon, and free returns statement. Cart abandonment rate dropped from 74% to 58%.

Mobile-First Fixes

74% of traffic was mobile. The mobile experience was an afterthought. Key changes: increased button size to minimum 48px touch targets, moved the CTA button to a fixed position at the bottom of mobile screens, and simplified the mobile navigation to reduce cognitive load.

What Moved the Needle Most

If we had to rank the 12 changes by impact, the top three were: (1) page load speed optimisation — this alone accounted for approximately 25% of the CVR improvement; (2) moving social proof above the fold — addressed the trust deficit immediately; (3) size guide simplification — this was the number one question in customer service tickets and removing that friction had an outsized impact on conversions.
