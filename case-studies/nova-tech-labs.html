Nova Tech Labs: MRR +280% and CAC -45% in 90 Days

How Zenkai Media helped Nova Tech Labs grow monthly recurring revenue by 280% while cutting customer acquisition costs by 45% in just 90 days.

The Challenge

Nova Tech Labs had strong product-market fit — their NPS was 72 and churn was under 3% — but their paid acquisition was bleeding money. They were running Google ads with no negative keyword strategy, a homepage as their landing page, and no LinkedIn presence despite selling to enterprise buyers. CAC was $840. LTV was $1,200. The math barely worked.

Our Solution

We rebuilt their paid acquisition from scratch. First, we separated their ICP (enterprise IT managers at companies 200–2000 employees) from the broader targeting they were running. We built LinkedIn campaigns targeting this ICP specifically, using case study and ROI-focused creative. On Google, we rebuilt the campaign structure around high-intent keywords only, added extensive negative lists, and built dedicated landing pages for each use case. We also implemented a free trial flow with a 5-email onboarding sequence to improve trial-to-paid conversion.

Results

By day 90: MRR had grown from $42k to $160k (+280%). CAC dropped from $840 to $462 (-45%). Free trial starts increased from 800 to 8,000 per month. Trial-to-paid conversion improved from 8% to 22% following the onboarding email sequence. Pipeline ROI of 3.2x on total ad spend.

Month-by-Month Breakdown

Month 1
CAC: $610 · MRR: $65k
ICP targeting rebuilt. LinkedIn campaigns launched. Homepage replaced with dedicated landing pages. Initial CAC reduction of 27%.
Month 2
CAC: $510 · MRR: $105k
Google campaign restructure complete. Negative keyword list added 2,400 terms. Trial-to-paid flow launched. MRR crosses $100k.
Month 3
CAC: $462 · MRR: $160k
LinkedIn retargeting added. Case study ads deployed for warm audiences. Trial conversion hits 22%. CAC at lowest point.

Key Learnings

The biggest win: Improving trial-to-paid conversion from 8% to 22% — without touching the product — accounted for 40% of total MRR growth. This required no additional ad spend. The email onboarding sequence was the single highest-ROI intervention of the entire engagement.

For B2B SaaS, the funnel doesn't end at signup. Most SaaS companies over-invest in top-of-funnel acquisition and under-invest in the activation layer. A user who signs up and doesn't activate is acquisition spend wasted. Fix activation before you scale acquisition.
