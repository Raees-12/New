How Luxe Apparel went from $200k to $1.2M revenue in 6 months

A struggling fashion e-commerce brand spending $30k/month on underperforming ads. A 1.8x ROAS that was barely breaking even. Six months later: 8.2x ROAS, 340% revenue growth, and a scalable marketing engine built to last.

The Situation

When Luxe Apparel came to us, they were a well-designed brand with a strong product and a loyal core customer base — but their paid advertising wasn't scaling. They were spending $30,000 per month on Meta ads and generating a 1.8x ROAS. After agency fees and cost of goods, they were barely breaking even on paid acquisition.

Their team had tried three different agencies in 18 months. Each one had optimised for the same metrics — CPM, click-through rate, cost per click — without looking at what was actually driving revenue. The problem wasn't the ad spend. It was the entire funnel.

What We Found in the Audit

Our first week is always a deep audit — we go inside every channel, every creative, every landing page, and every data source before we touch a single thing. For Luxe, we found five critical issues:

  1. No audience segmentation. They were running one campaign targeting a broad 18–45 female audience. No distinction between cold audiences, retargeting, and existing customers. All three require completely different creative and messaging strategies.
  2. Generic creative with no hook strategy. Every ad opened with a product shot and a logo. There was no engineered reason to stop scrolling. Average video view rate: 4.2%. Industry standard for winning creatives: 15–25%.
  3. Landing pages converting at 0.8%. Visitors were clicking the ads but abandoning immediately. The product pages had weak photography, no social proof above the fold, and a confusing size guide. The best ad in the world can't fix a 0.8% conversion rate.
  4. Zero post-purchase flow. Existing customers — the cheapest audience to market to — were receiving no targeted communication. There was no loyalty mechanism, no repeat purchase incentive, and no referral structure.
  5. Best-sellers buried. Their top 3 products (which accounted for 60% of organic revenue) were not being featured in any paid campaign. All ad spend was going to new launches that hadn't proven organic demand.

The core insight: Luxe didn't have an advertising problem. They had a funnel problem. The ads were fine. Everything the traffic landed on was broken.

The Strategy: Full-Funnel Rebuild

We presented a 90-day rebuild plan with three phases. The goal of Phase 1 was to stop the bleeding — cut wasteful spend and fix conversion rate. Phase 2 was to find the winning formula. Phase 3 was to scale it aggressively.

Phase 1 · Days 1–30
Stop the Bleed — Fix Conversion Rate

We immediately paused all underperforming ad sets and reduced active campaigns to only their top three best-selling products. We rebuilt the product pages — new photography brief, social proof (reviews) moved above the fold, size guide simplified to a single image, and checkout friction reduced by removing unnecessary fields. Landing page conversion rate moved from 0.8% to 2.4% within three weeks — before we changed a single ad.

Phase 2 · Days 31–60
Find the Winning Formula — Creative Testing

We restructured the Meta campaigns into three funnel stages: cold (new audiences), warm (website visitors and video viewers), and hot (add-to-cart abandonments and past purchasers). Each stage received different creative — cold got educational and inspirational content, warm got social proof and product demos, hot got urgency-driven direct response. We produced 24 new ad creatives and tested two hooks per creative. By day 60, we had identified four "winning" creatives with CPMs under $8 and a click-through rate above 3.5%.

Phase 3 · Days 61–180
Scale Aggressively — Double Down on Winners

With proven creatives and a landing page converting at 3.1%, we increased ad spend by 40% in week 9. We also launched a Klaviyo email flow for post-purchase customers — a 5-email sequence designed to generate reviews, encourage repeat purchase, and drive referrals. This single flow added 18% incremental revenue on top of the paid acquisition results. By month 6, ROAS had peaked at 8.2x and monthly revenue had crossed $1.2M.

What Made the Difference

When we debrief on any campaign, we always identify the 20% of work that drove 80% of results. For Luxe, three interventions drove the majority of the improvement:

  • Landing page CRO. Moving conversion rate from 0.8% to 3.1% tripled the effective value of every dollar spent on ads — without touching the ad spend. This single change accounted for approximately 35% of total revenue improvement.
  • Audience segmentation. Splitting cold, warm, and hot audiences — and using different creative for each — dramatically improved relevance scores, reduced CPMs, and increased purchase intent at each funnel stage.
  • Post-purchase email flow. The 18% incremental revenue from the email flow cost almost nothing to produce. Existing customers convert at 5x the rate of new customers and require no paid acquisition cost. Every brand that ignores their email list is leaving money on the table.

Key lesson: Most brands try to fix ad performance by changing their ads. In our experience, the bigger wins are almost always in the landing page, the offer structure, and the post-purchase experience. Fix the funnel before you scale the spend.
